# BriefingClaw Marketing Plan — Stage 2: Content & Messaging Strategy

## Core Value Proposition

### Primary Positioning
**"Know everything. Read nothing."**

BriefingClaw is the AI assistant for intelligence — curated briefings, AI summaries, custom topics, delivered when you want.

### Value Proposition Statement
> You wake up to 47 newsletters and 23 news alerts. By the time you've read what matters, half your morning is gone.
> 
> Connect BriefingClaw and get an AI that:
> - Curates briefings from your chosen sources
> - Synthesizes what matters to you
> - Delivers when you want, how you want
> - Tracks topics, industries, and keywords important to you
> - Runs locally — your reading habits stay private

## X/Twitter / LinkedIn Content Strategy

### Account Setup
- **Handle:** @BriefingClaw
- **Bio:** "AI for intelligence. Curated briefings. AI summaries. Custom topics. briefingclaw.com"
- **Profile Image:** BriefingClaw logo (professional, news aesthetic)
- **Header:** Intelligence/briefing visualization

### Posting Cadence
- **3-4 posts per day**
- **Best times:** 6-8 AM EST (morning briefing), 12-2 PM EST, 6-8 PM EST
- **Focus:** Time savings, intelligence curation, being informed

### Content Pillars

#### Pillar 1: Information Overload (35%)
47 newsletters, too much to read, morning chaos.

#### Pillar 2: Curation (30%)
AI summaries, custom topics, what matters.

#### Pillar 3: Efficiency (25%)
3 hours to 15 minutes, know everything without reading everything.

#### Pillar 4: Privacy (10%)
Reading habits stay private.

### Sample Posts

**Overload:**
> 47 newsletters. 23 news alerts.
> 
> You're drowning in information. Starving for insight.
> 
> BriefingClaw: 15 minutes. Everything that matters.
> 
> Know everything. Read nothing.

**Curation:**
> "What's important in my industry today?"
> 
> → scroll through 50 sources
> 
> → BriefingClaw: "Here's your 10-minute briefing"
> 
> Curated. Synthesized. Delivered.

**Efficiency:**
> 3 hours of reading → 15 minutes of briefing.
> 
> Same insight. Fraction of the time.

**Privacy:**
> Your reading habits reveal:
> - Your interests
> - Your competitors
> - Your strategy
> 
> Keep it private.
